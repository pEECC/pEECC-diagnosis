Paediatric EECC Diagnostic Tool

About this tool

Many children admitted to hospital have critical illness: severe illness where early recognition and timely treatment can prevent death or disability.

Hospitals need to be prepared to respond when these children arrive, and to continue the care they require throughout their hospital journey. This requires equipment and workforce, but also people and systems organised around a common goal.

This tool helps hospital leaders and clinical teams diagnose what is needed.

Step 1: Resources

Assess equipment, supplies, space, and workforce at hospital level and in each ward or unit caring for sick children.

Step 2: Environment

Assess whether your hospital's work environment supports staff to care for sick children.

Step 3: Dashboard

Review the results.

Step 4: Interpretation

Use the results to identify practical priorities for local action.

Step 5: Prioritise, design and adapt interventions

Use the resource and environment diagnosis to prioritise, design, adapt, and sequence a small number of locally feasible interventions.

6. Interpretation - environment

What do the environment scores suggest?

These results help explain the conditions, beyond resources and workforce, that are needed for responsive care of the critically ill child. Legitimacy refers to whether staff feel allowed and expected to act, and whether emergency care is prioritised. Capability refers to staff confidence, competence, and practical ability to recognise and respond. Connectedness refers to how people — clinical and non-clinical staff, patients, and caregivers — come together, share information, and work towards common goals. These aspects are needed throughout the child's hospital journey.
